Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 23, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Nikita Lesnikov ("we", "our", "us") handles information in connection with the mobile game Orbmorph: Tap & Evolve ("the App"). By installing or using the App you agree to the practices described in this policy.

1. Information We Collect

The App itself does not ask you to register, log in, or provide any personal information directly. However, certain third-party services integrated into the App may automatically collect limited data as described below.

1.1 Data Stored Locally on Your Device

  • Game progress, high scores, unlocked skins, and in-game settings are stored locally on your device using the platform's standard storage APIs.
  • This data is not transmitted to any server controlled by us.
  • You can delete this data at any time by uninstalling the App.

1.2 Data Collected by Third-Party Services

The App integrates the following third-party services, which may collect data according to their own privacy policies:

  • Google AdMob — serves advertisements and may collect a device advertising identifier (IDFA on iOS), IP address, approximate location derived from IP, and basic device and ad-interaction information for purposes of ad delivery, measurement, and fraud prevention.
  • Google Firebase (Analytics, Crashlytics, Performance Monitoring, Firestore) — used for usage analytics, crash reporting, performance measurement, and storing online leaderboard scores. Firebase may collect a Firebase Installation ID, basic device information (model, OS version, language, country), app usage events (screen views, session duration, app opens), crash logs, performance metrics, and any player-supplied nickname submitted to the leaderboard. Firebase data is processed according to the Firebase Privacy and Security Policy.
  • In-App Purchases — processed by the platform's official in-app purchase system. Payment details are handled entirely by the platform provider. We never see or store your payment information.
  • Platform game services (for example, Apple Game Center) — handle leaderboards and achievements. If you sign in with your platform account, your gamer profile, scores, and achievements may be shared with the respective service according to their terms. Sign-in is optional.
  • System platform services — required platform components used to run the services above.

2. How We Use the Information

The limited data collected by third-party services is used to:

  • Display advertisements (including personalized ads where permitted by you and applicable law).
  • Process and verify in-app purchases.
  • Save and display your scores on global leaderboards (only if you opt in to platform game services, or submit a nickname to the in-app leaderboard).
  • Measure app usage, identify crashes, and improve performance and stability.
  • Detect and prevent fraud, abuse, and technical issues.
  • Comply with legal obligations.

We ourselves do not build advertising profiles, sell your data, or use it for purposes other than operating the App.

3. Advertising

The App displays ads provided by Google AdMob. AdMob may use your device's advertising identifier and other limited information to deliver more relevant ads.

You can control ad personalization at any time through your device settings:

  • iOS: Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking → toggle off "Allow Apps to Request to Track". You can also decline the App Tracking Transparency prompt the first time the App requests it.
  • On other platforms, you can opt out of ad personalization or reset your advertising identifier through the device's privacy or ads settings.

For more information, see the Google Ads Privacy Policy and the AdMob Help Center.

4. Analytics, Crash Reporting, and Performance Monitoring

We use Google Firebase Analytics, Firebase Crashlytics, and Firebase Performance Monitoring to understand how the App is used, identify crashes, and improve overall performance and stability.

These services may collect:

  • App events such as launches, screen views, session duration, and in-game actions (for example, starting or finishing a run).
  • A Firebase Installation ID, used to distinguish individual app installations. This identifier is reset when the App is uninstalled.
  • Basic device information, including device model, operating system version, language, and country (derived from IP address).
  • Crash reports, including stack traces, device state at the time of the crash, and the version of the App.
  • Performance metrics, such as app start time, frozen frames, and network request timing.

This data is used solely to maintain and improve the App. We do not use it for advertising purposes.

For more details, see the Firebase Privacy and Security Policy.

5. Online Leaderboard

The App offers an optional online leaderboard powered by Google Cloud Firestore. If you choose to submit your score, the App stores your self-chosen nickname and your best score on Google's servers so that other players can see the global ranking.

  • No real name, email address, or other personally identifying information is required.
  • You can choose any nickname you like; you do not have to use your real name.
  • You can stop submitting scores at any time by not entering a nickname.
  • If you would like your nickname and score removed from the leaderboard, please contact us at the email below.

6. In-App Purchases

The App offers optional in-app purchases (for example, cosmetic skin packs) processed exclusively through the platform's official in-app purchase system. We do not collect, store, or have access to your credit card, bank account, or any other payment information. All purchases are subject to the applicable platform's terms of service.

7. Platform Leaderboards and Achievements

If you choose to sign in with the platform's game services (for example, Apple Game Center), your public gamer profile (gamer name, avatar) and your in-game scores and achievements may be uploaded to the platform's servers and displayed on public leaderboards. This is an optional feature.

  • To manage Game Center: iOS Settings → [your name] → Game Center.
  • On other platforms, you can manage or revoke game-service access through your account's permission settings.

8. Children's Privacy

The App is not directed to children under the age of 13 (or under 16 in the European Economic Area, or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information through the App, please contact us and we will take reasonable steps to delete it.

9. Your Rights (GDPR / UK GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • The right to access the data we hold about you.
  • The right to request correction or deletion of your data.
  • The right to restrict or object to processing.
  • The right to data portability.
  • The right to withdraw consent at any time.
  • The right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

Because we do not collect personal data ourselves, most data requests should be directed to the respective third-party service. For requests concerning the in-app leaderboard or any other matter, contact us at the address below.

10. Your Rights (CCPA — California Residents)

If you are a California resident, you have the right to know what personal information is collected, the right to request deletion, and the right not to be discriminated against for exercising your rights. We do not sell personal information.

11. Data Retention

Data stored locally on your device remains until you delete it or uninstall the App. Data handled by third-party services is retained according to their respective retention policies. Leaderboard entries (nickname and score) are retained until you request deletion by contacting us.

12. Permissions Used by the App

iOS

  • App Tracking Transparency (ATT) — on iOS 14.5 and above, the App will ask for your permission before allowing AdMob to access the IDFA for cross-app tracking. You may decline at any time.
  • Network access — required to load advertisements, process purchases, and submit leaderboard scores.
